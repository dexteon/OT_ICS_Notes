Operational Technology (OT) Security Program

1. What Is a Risk Assessment?

  • Systematic identification of assets, threats, and vulnerabilities in OT systems
  • Scoring of likelihood and impact to prioritize what matters most
  • Control design aligned to NIST 800-82 and ISA 62443 standards
  • Residual risk decision: Mitigate, accept, transfer, or avoid

2. Why It Matters for Verizon

  • Mission-Critical: Power & cooling disruptions impact 8+ NECs nationwide
  • Safety: BAS failures can affect employee/public safety
  • Compliance: NERC CIP, NIST CSF, ISA 62443 expectations
  • Ransomware: 87% increase in OT-targeted attacks (2024)
  • Operational Visibility: No loss of control during containment

3. NIST SP 800-82: ICS Security Guide

  • Standard for Operational Technology (OT) – mandatory for critical infrastructure
  • Risk Management Framework (RMF): Categorize → Select → Implement → Monitor
  • Unique OT Considerations: Safety, availability, operational constraints
  • Defense-in-Depth: Multi-layer controls (technical, administrative, physical)

4. ISA/IEC 62443: Security Levels

SL 1–2

Basic protection; unintentional misuse

SL 3

Moderate resources; sophisticated attacks

SL 4

Fortress-level; extensive APT capabilities

Target: NEC critical systems → SL 3+; PLCs with no patch path → SL 2 + compensating controls

7. Key OT Hazards (Your Environment)

  • Internet-exposed BAS/VPN: Vendor access paths + RDP into L1/L2
  • Unpatched Controllers: KEV-listed BAS systems, legacy firmware
  • Shared/Default Credentials: No CyberArk enforcement yet on some sites
  • Weak Segmentation: BAS <→ IT network, no Modbus firewall rules
  • Backup Gaps: Live backups only; no offline restore tests

8. Risk Scoring: Likelihood × Impact

Likelihood (1–5)

1 = Rare
3 = Possible
5 = Certain

Impact (1–5)

1 = Negligible
3 = Moderate
5 = Severe (safety)

Score = L × I

1–5 = Low | 6–10 = Medium | 11–15 = High | 16–25 = Critical

9. Control Categories (NIST 800-82)

  • Segmentation: OT DMZ, firewall rules, Modbus allow-list
  • Access Control: Remove defaults, CyberArk vaulting, MFA for remote
  • Vulnerability Management: Patch SLA, compensating controls for EOL assets
  • Monitoring/Detection: Splunk SIEM, baselines, anomaly alerts
  • Backup & Recovery: Offline air-gapped backups, restore tests, RTO/RPO

10. Risk Disposition: 4 Paths

Mitigate

Implement controls to reduce risk to acceptable level

Accept

Document, approve, monitor (limited to low residual only)

Transfer

Vendor SLA, insurance, or contractual shift

Avoid

Decommission asset or eliminate exposure
